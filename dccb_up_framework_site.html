Field Context

Uttar Pradesh: The Cooperative Credit Heartland

Uttar Pradesh has 50 DCCBs — the largest state concentration in India — serving over 12 crore rural households across 75 districts. Yet field diagnostics reveal a stark gap: CBS has been deployed, but the operational reality remains heavily manual.

From Lucknow to Varanasi, from the sugarcane belt of eastern UP to the wheat fields of western UP, DCCBs serve millions of Kisan Credit Card holders whose loan applications take 14–42 days, whose NPA classification happens in handwritten registers, and whose CRAR calculations live in Excel spreadsheets on one officer's desktop.

The NABARD–SSPL framework is specifically calibrated to the institutional reality of UP's cooperative banking sector — most UP DCCBs fall in the PD-RFI (Partially Digitalized) category, with the priority being not new technology procurement but completing and optimising what already exists.

Part I · Section 1
The Generic Bank Product Effectiveness Model (GBPEM)

A 5-axis analytical framework replacing aspiration-driven procurement with evidence-based product prioritisation for Rural Financial Institutions.

GBPEM Core Principle: A product scoring below 15/25 on the composite matrix should not be independently implemented by a DCCB. Products scoring 18–25 represent the priority set. This framework prevents DCCBs from procuring large-bank technology (SBI YONO cost: ₹4,000 crore) that delivers zero rural value.
🏆
PMS
Product Maturity Score
How proven is this product at large-bank scale? Score 1 = experimental; 5 = 100M+ user deployment with documented ROI.
🌾
RAS
Rural Adaptability Score
Does it work on 2G USSD, feature phones, with illiterate users, via BC agents? Score 5 = AePS (biometric only, no smartphone needed).
⚙️
ICI
Implementation Complexity Index
Score 5 = deployable in 90 days under ₹25 lakhs with no dedicated IT team. Score 1 = 18+ months, ₹5 crore+, RBI approvals needed.
💰
FIQ
Financial Impact Quotient
Score 5 = 50%+ cost reduction or significant revenue uplift, break-even under 18 months. Uses verified NABARD benchmarks only.
🧑‍🌾
CVDS
Client Value Delivery Score
Does it genuinely serve UP farmers, SHG members, PACS members? Score 1 = urban-biased product. Score 5 = solves acute rural pain point.

Section 02

Three-Category RFI Classification Framework

Every product recommendation, workflow, and roadmap in this framework is differentiated by RFI category. All 50 UP DCCBs map to one of these three categories.

🌟
Category 1 · DM-RFI
Digitally Mature RFI
~4 UP DCCBs · 55–70 nationally · 15–20% of total
All 10 conditions must be met:
Full CBS at 100% branches — real-time, not batch mode
Active UPI endpoint >1,000 monthly transactions for 12+ months
RuPay card issued to >60% of savings account holders
Aadhaar eKYC operational at ALL branch points
PACS ERP integration live for >50% of PACS
NACH/eMandate for >30% of loan accounts
MIS reporting within 3 working days of period-end
Dedicated IT officer on staff (not vendor-dependent)
SSPL Posture: "Optimisation and Advanced AI Integration" — API enablement, satellite NDVI credit scoring, PACS 100% completion, FPO supply chain finance, SSPL SOC.
⚡
Category 2 · PD-RFI
Partially Digitalised RFI
~32 UP DCCBs · 195–210 nationally · 55–60% of total
Has some, lacks others:
✓ CBS live at HQ and most branches; some still offline
✓ Aadhaar eKYC at HQ minimum; NOT at PACS level
✓ RuPay card issued to 30–60% of savings holders
✗ No functional UPI endpoint or mobile banking channel
✗ PACS ERP <50% linked; significant data quality issues
✗ NPA tracking: periodic manual Excel reviews only
✗ CRAR/SLR: still fully Excel-based with no automation
✗ No active BC agent micro-ATM network
SSPL Posture: "Completion and Consolidation" — highest-volume SSPL work. Phase 1: PACS data quality, seasonal NACH, BC micro-ATM rollout, semi-automated compliance.
🌱
Category 3 · PDC-RFI
Pre-Digital Commencing
~14 UP DCCBs · 85–90 nationally · ~25% of total
Limiting conditions:
CBS at HQ but <60% of branches live and operational
OR CBS live in batch mode only — no real-time inter-branch
No Aadhaar eKYC deployed anywhere
No UPI, mobile banking, or digital payment channel
RuPay card below 20% of savings holders
All PACS on paper registers — zero digital linkage
Internet unreliable at 40%+ of branches
CRAR/SLR entirely manual — no CBS data extract at all
SSPL Posture: "Foundation First" — CBS stabilisation → staff literacy → eKYC → AePS → basic compliance. Some will need 5–7 years to reach DM-RFI.

Product 13 · Internal Operations

Regulatory Compliance Engine — CRAR · SLR · NABARD OSS

The highest-ROI internal product for any UP DCCB. Current state: 780+ manual person-hours per cycle. Target state: 90 hours with automated CBS-to-ENSURE pipeline.

CRAR Formula
CRAR = (T1 + T2) ÷ RWA × 100
Minimum: 9% (RBI mandate for cooperative banks)
T1: Paid-up capital + Reserves + Retained earnings
T2: Subordinated debt + Revaluation reserves
RWA Risk Weights: G-Secs 0% · AAA bonds 20% · Retail loans 75–100% · NPAs 150%
SLR Formula
SLR = SLR Securities ÷ NDTL × 100
Minimum: 18.00% of NDTL (computed fortnightly)
SLR Alert: Trigger at 18.50% (before hitting 18% floor)
CRR: 4.50% of NDTL as cash with RBI (daily average)
Automation: CBS investment module → real-time ratio display → instant breach alert

NPA Early Warning System — Green · Orange · Red

Target: 20–25% NPA formation reduction. Pre-NPA accounts intervened at Orange stage recover at 78% vs 42% at Red stage. This difference is the economic case for EWS deployment.

🟢
GREEN — No Action
• All repayments on schedule (0 DPD)
• KCC utilisation within seasonal range
• NDVI reading in seasonal average
• NACH mandate clearing normally
• No insurance claim in crop zone
Recovery probability: >95%
🟡
ORANGE — Intervene in 14 Days
• Repayment delayed 5–30 days (1–30 DPD)
• Account utilisation drop >20% from previous month
• NDVI drop >15% below seasonal average
• NACH mandate bounced once
• No field officer contact in 30+ days
Recovery probability: 78% with intervention
🔴
RED — Immediate Escalation
• Repayment delayed 30+ days (approaching 90 DPD NPA)
• KCC account dormant (zero utilisation 60+ days)
• NDVI confirming crop failure in zone
• NACH bounce two+ consecutive months
• Cluster signal: 5+ accounts same PACS defaulting
Recovery probability: 42% at this stage

Kisan Credit Card — revolving crop credit product for Indian farmers. Anchor product of every DCCB. Current UP DCCB TAT: 14–42 days. Digital LOS target: 2–5 days. 8–12% NPA rate currently; AI scoring targets 3–5%.
LOS+
Loan Origination System — digital workflow managing complete application-to-disbursal process. Integrates Aadhaar eKYC, Bhulekh land records, PM Kisan DB, CIBIL, satellite NDVI, weather API, and PMFBY auto-enrol.
NACH+
National Automated Clearing House — NPCI bulk payment for EMI/mandate-based debit. Critical UP adaptation: seasonal variable-date mandate aligned to kharif (Oct–Nov) and rabi (Apr–May) harvest cycles.
NABARD+
National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development — apex development bank for rural India. Infused ₹42,000+ crore into cooperative banks in FY 2024–25. Operates OSS (Off-Site Surveillance System) for cooperative bank monitoring.
NDVI+
Normalised Difference Vegetation Index — satellite-derived crop health metric from ISRO NRSC. Used in EWS: NDVI drop >15% below seasonal average triggers Orange alert. Used in AI credit scoring for agri PD estimation.
NPA+
Non-Performing Asset — loan with principal/interest unpaid for 90+ days. Current UP DCCB KCC NPA rate: 8–12%. EWS target: reduce to 4–6% (DM-RFI). Pre-NPA intervention at Orange stage recovers 78% vs 42% at NPA.
PACS+
Primary Agricultural Credit Society — village-level cooperative; last-mile credit delivery. 63,000 functional PACS nationally. In UP, PACS data arrives at DCCBs on paper — the most critical data quality gap to solve.
PMFBY+
Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana — crop insurance scheme. Government subsidy covers 85%+ of premium. Farmer pays only 1.5–2% of sum insured. DM-RFI auto-enrolment: 98%+ at KCC disbursal. Current UP rate: <40%.
RFI+
Rural Financial Institution — the DCCB category designation in this framework. Three categories: DM-RFI (Digitally Mature, ~4 UP DCCBs), PD-RFI (Partially Digitalized, ~32 UP DCCBs), PDC-RFI (Pre-Digital, ~14 UP DCCBs).
SLR+
Statutory Liquidity Ratio = SLR Securities / NDTL × 100. Minimum 18.00% of NDTL (RBI, 2024). SSPL compliance engine triggers alert at 18.50% — before the 18% floor is breached. Currently computed manually in Excel in most UP DCCBs.
SSPL+
Sahakar Sarathi Pvt Ltd — NABARD's shared services entity backed by Ministry of Cooperation + NCDC. Targets 130 million cooperative banking customers. Expected ₹1,500 crore annual savings from Year 5. Serves all 352 DCCBs.
UPI+
Unified Payments Interface — NPCI real-time payment protocol. 10 billion+ monthly transactions (2024). For UP rural clients: entry via USSD *99# (feature phone, no data) → AePS → UPI QR at PACS → UPI 123PAY → full mobile UPI.
USSD+
Unstructured Supplementary Service Data — *99# banking on any GSM feature phone without internet. Zero data cost. Supports balance inquiry, mini-statement, fund transfer. First digital product for all PDC-RFI DCCBs. Deploy in 30–45 days.
V-CIP+
Video Customer Identification Process — RBI-approved remote KYC via video call. Enables rural account opening without branch visit. Critical for migrant worker accounts (city worker opens home-DCCB account remotely). DM-RFI only initially.
ADEI+
Annual Digital Effectiveness Index — SSPL/NABARD composite digital maturity score (0–100) published annually per DCCB. PDC-RFI: below 30. PD-RFI: 30–60. DM-RFI: above 60. Used for peer benchmarking and category upgrade decisions.
e-NWR+
Electronic Negotiable Warehouse Receipt — WDRA-issued digital receipt enabling post-harvest collateral lending. Farmer deposits grain → e-NWR issued → DCCB lends 70–80% of market value → farmer repays after selling at better price. Eliminates distress selling.
BC+
Business Correspondent — DCCB-appointed agent providing doorstep banking. Monthly income potential (active BC): ₹10,950–18,450. Device: smartphone + micro-ATM (₹12,000–15,000). Services: AePS, BBPS, account opening, KCC application assistance.
DM-RFI+
Digitally Mature RFI — Category 1. ~4 UP DCCBs. 55–70 nationally. All 10 CBS criteria met. SSPL posture: AI integration, satellite NDVI, FPO finance, SSPL SOC. Primary gap: analytics layer and PACS 100% completion.
PD-RFI+
Partially Digitalized RFI — Category 2. ~32 UP DCCBs. 195–210 nationally. CBS live but underutilised. SSPL posture: Completion and Consolidation. Priority: PACS data quality, seasonal NACH, BC micro-ATM, semi-automated compliance.
PDC-RFI+
Pre-Digital Commencing RFI — Category 3. ~14 UP DCCBs. 85–90 nationally. CBS partial or batch. SSPL posture: Foundation First. CBS stabilisation → staff literacy → Aadhaar eKYC → AePS → basic compliance automation.

⚠️ The "Do Not" List — Products NOT Recommended for Direct DCCB Implementation

❌ Custom superapp like YONO — ₹4,000 crore development. Use SSPL white-label at ₹15–30L for 65% parity at 0.05% of cost.
❌ Standalone TMS for treasury — CBS investment module covers 100% of DCCB treasury needs. Bloomberg-level TMS is irrelevant.
❌ Dedicated AML for PDC-RFI — ₹10–30L/year unnecessary. Manual CTR/STR + SSPL shared AML (₹1–3L/year) is sufficient.
❌ Own ATM at every branch — ₹15–40 crore for 50-branch DCCB. Shared ATM + micro-ATMs deliver equivalent coverage at 10% cost.
❌ Derivatives / forex capability — Outside cooperative bank regulatory scope and business model entirely.
❌ ML credit scoring for PDC-RFI before clean data — Garbage data = garbage model. 24 months of clean CBS history is the prerequisite.
